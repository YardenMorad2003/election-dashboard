Israeli Elections — Support

MCP connector for Israeli national election data, 1992–2022

What this connector does

It lets an AI assistant (ChatGPT, Claude, or any MCP client) query a research dataset of Israeli national elections: locality-level results for all thirteen Knesset elections from 1992 to 2022, the 1996/1999/2001 direct prime-ministerial contests, party histories, bloc-level vote-transfer estimates (ecological inference and INES survey data), and CBS socio-economic profiles. Localities and parties can be asked about in Hebrew or English.

The same dataset powers the interactive Israeli Elections Dashboard. Sources: official Central Elections Committee locality results; CBS socio-economic municipal data (2021); Israel National Election Studies survey series (1996–2022).

Example questions

  • "How did Bnei Brak vote in 2022, party by party?"
  • "Where did left-bloc voters go between 2021 and 2022 — ballot estimate vs the INES survey?"
  • "Compare Umm al-Fahm's turnout in the 1999 and 2001 prime-ministerial elections."
  • "Show Likud's seat trajectory since 1992 and its strongholds."
  • "What's the socio-economic profile of Rahat, and how did it vote in 2020?"

Connecting

The MCP endpoint is https://israeli-elections-mcp.yardenmorad2003.workers.dev/mcp (Streamable HTTP, no authentication). Add it as a custom connector / plugin in your assistant's settings.

Troubleshooting

  • A number looks wrong — percentages are shares of valid votes; vote counts marked votes_est are estimates derived from official shares. Please report anything that disagrees with official Central Elections Committee figures.
  • A locality isn't found — try the Hebrew spelling or the CBS semel code; very small localities and Bedouin tribes are unmapped in some elections.
  • The connector doesn't respond — the service is stateless and usually recovers immediately; try again, then report it if the problem persists.
